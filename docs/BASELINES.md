# Baseline comparison

> Subset numbers. Generated by `panelcast compare --baselines` on the ~5,000-album
> AOTY subset — the same real splits the model is evaluated on (see
> [`MODEL_CARD.md`](../MODEL_CARD.md) → *Real-data subset re-baseline*). Model rows
> are from the 2026-07-03 re-baseline under the 0.6.0 defaults (`offset_logit`,
> the `gbm_offset` stacking block, genre pooling auto-on — #85/#86). **Block
> state**: the feature matrix now includes the `gbm_offset` column by default, so
> the ridge/GBM baselines consume it too — every row in these tables sees the
> same X. Full-corpus validation is tracked in
> [#15](https://github.com/cupidthatbtc/panelcast/issues/15).

This page states, in the open, **where the hierarchical Bayesian model actually
sits against simple baselines** — because that placement is the honest argument for
what the model is *for*. (`reports/baselines/` is gitignored, so the generated
table is otherwise invisible in the repo.)

## Within-entity temporal holdout — predict an entity's next event (N = 653)

| Model | MAE | RMSE | R² | CRPS | 80% cov | 95% cov | 95% width |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **panelcast (Bayesian)** | **5.30** | **7.65** | **0.501** | **3.87** | 0.853 | 0.963 | 31.5 |
| ridge | 5.38 | 7.68 | 0.498 | 4.02 | 0.879 | 0.965 | 32.2 |
| gradient boosting | 5.58 | 7.88 | 0.471 | 4.12 | 0.763 | 0.888 | 22.6 |
| conformal GBM | 5.63 | 8.01 | 0.453 | 4.15 | 0.821 | 0.954 | 33.6 |
| last score | 6.10 | 8.88 | 0.328 | 4.71 | 0.900 | 0.971 | 39.4 |
| entity mean | 6.11 | 8.92 | 0.322 | 4.59 | 0.818 | 0.925 | 29.8 |
| global mean | 7.23 | 10.91 | −0.015 | 5.55 | 0.890 | 0.956 | 43.3 |

Read without flattery:

- **The model now leads the table** — MAE, RMSE, R², and CRPS at once, with
  calibrated intervals. This is the 0.6.0 `gbm_offset` stacking block doing
  exactly what it was built for (#86): the boosted offset carries the
  temporal/album-type/critic-history point signal into the mean as one more
  covariate (out-of-fold on train rows), and the structured model keeps
  everything else. The pre-0.6.0 story — "a flexible regressor extracts more
  point signal than the structured model" — no longer holds on this split.
- **The lead over ridge is real but modest** (5.30 vs 5.38 MAE, 0.501 vs
  0.498 R²); the decisive gap is CRPS (3.87 vs 4.02) and the modeled intervals.
  Note the baselines also consume the `gbm_offset` column now — ridge improved
  from 5.62 to 5.38 for the same reason the model did. The raw GBM *worsened*
  (5.41 → 5.58): feeding a GBM its own stacked prediction is redundancy, not
  information.
- **It clears the naive entity baselines by a wide margin.** R² 0.501 vs the
  per-entity mean's 0.322 and persistence's 0.328.
- **The GBM's residual intervals remain over-confident** (0.763 / 0.888
  coverage); the model's are modeled, near-nominal (0.853 / 0.963), and now
  also the *narrowest* calibrated ones (31.5 vs conformal's 33.6 and ridge's
  32.2).

The model's justification used to be "not the leaderboard — calibrated
intervals, variance decomposition, a generative story." Those deliverables all
still hold; as of 0.6.0 the leaderboard argument holds too.

> The conformal check ([#50](https://github.com/cupidthatbtc/panelcast/issues/50))
> still stands as the honest control: **conformal GBM** wraps the same GBM in
> split-conformal calibration and reaches near-nominal coverage (95%: 0.954;
> 80%: 0.821) — calibration alone is cheap. But under the 0.6.0 baseline it no
> longer matches the model on anything else: point accuracy (5.63 vs 5.30),
> CRPS (4.15 vs 3.87), and interval width (33.6 vs 31.5) all favor the model.
> What conformal cannot produce remains what it could never produce: a
> generative story you can interrogate (posterior parameters, PPC) and the
> entity-vs-residual variance decomposition.

## Cold-start — a brand-new entity with no history (artist-disjoint, N = 799)

| Model | MAE | R² | 80% cov | 95% cov | 95% width |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **panelcast (Bayesian)** | **6.91** | **0.113** | 0.851 | 0.962 | 43.4 |
| ridge | 7.21 | 0.076 | 0.796 | 0.916 | 31.8 |
| conformal GBM | 7.22 | 0.086 | 0.723 | 0.920 | 32.2 |
| gradient boosting | 7.34 | 0.060 | 0.603 | 0.786 | 21.1 |
| entity mean | 7.69 | −0.001 | 0.743 | 0.900 | 29.1 |
| last score | 7.69 | −0.001 | 0.837 | 0.947 | 38.0 |
| global mean | 7.69 | −0.001 | 0.901 | 0.957 | 44.0 |

The model **leads this table outright** (6.91 / 0.113) and is the only method
with 95% coverage at nominal (0.962); its cold-start intervals are
appropriately *wider* than the within-entity ones (43.4 vs 31.5). Two 0.6.0
promotions stack here: the genre pooling tier (`entity_group_pooling`, #85 —
new entities from a seen group start at the group level, worth MAE ≈ −0.1 and
R² ≈ +0.02 at every screened seed) and the `gbm_offset` covariate (real
leakage-safe values on this evaluation split via the feature parquets; only
hypothetical-entity CLI predictions degrade to the train-mean offset).

Read without flattery, still: R² 0.113 is weak in absolute terms — debut
reception remains close to unpredictable from the available features (the
covariates-only headroom estimate was ≈ 0.083,
[`.audit/genre_pooling/`](../.audit/genre_pooling/covariates_only_r2.md), and
the pooling tier captured essentially all of the genre share of it), and every
method's MAE sits within ~0.8 of the global mean's. The conformal wrapper's
exchangeability guarantee is false by construction for never-seen entities and
degrades accordingly (0.920 at 95%), though it still beats the raw GBM's
0.786. The multi-seed screening and publication-scale confirmation behind the
pooling promotion live in
[`.audit/genre_pooling/`](../.audit/genre_pooling/gate_on_screening.md);
domains without a usable group column resolve the gate to off automatically.

## R² is an average over a steep history gradient

The within-entity R² ≈ 0.50 hides a strong dependence on how much history an entity
has:

| Train events | n | R² | RMSE | 95% cov |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1–2 | 124 | 0.530 | 5.84 | 0.976 |
| 3–5 | 262 | 0.498 | 6.76 | 0.973 |
| 6–10 | 180 | 0.415 | 8.69 | 0.956 |
| 11+ | 87 | 0.525 | 9.83 | 0.931 |

RMSE still roughly doubles from thin to deep catalogs — prolific entities have
idiosyncratic, higher-variance trajectories that are harder to predict in
absolute terms. But the pre-0.6.0 *R² decline* with catalog depth (0.52 → 0.33)
is gone: the 11+ bin jumped from 0.334 to 0.525, the largest single-stratum
gain from the `gbm_offset` block, which is where the exogenous
temporal/album-type signal it carries matters most relative to a long entity
history. The headline R² remains conditional on the holdout's history mix (and
on the minimum-history / minimum-ratings selection filter) — report the
gradient, not the single number.

## Reproduce

```bash
panelcast run --stages splits,features   # if the split/feature artifacts aren't present
panelcast compare --baselines            # writes reports/baselines/ (gitignored)
```

`reports/` is gitignored (large, regenerable), so this page is the curated,
version-controlled copy. The committed snapshot of the model's own evaluation lives
at `.audit/baseline_metrics.json`, regenerated from the current default model
(`user_score_20260703_121349.nc`, the 2026-07-03 re-baseline under the 0.6.0
defaults: `offset_logit`, `gbm_offset`, genre pooling auto-on).
